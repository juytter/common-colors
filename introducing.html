Color names is a strange and funny area. Especially on Web.
There is no problem picturing approximately a Yellow or a Red but ...

What about the DodgerBlue ? It is a reference to the blue of Dodgers. You had to know it. Did you know that Darkgrey is actually lighter than Gray ? Chartreuse ? A French green liqueur that almost nobody has been able to see (and drink !) for years in France. Papayawhip sounds very colorful but in fact, no. Snow is not exactly white and a warm color... Seriously ?

We have a sensory relationship to colors, there are millions and with different meanings depending on culture. Ask ten people to choose a Blue color, they will pick ten different Blue.

The story of DodgerBlue is self-explanatory : many web color names have been chosen arbitrarily, do not evoke anything for the rest of the world, or worse, are misleading.

To make everyone agree, it's simple, we no longer use names but color codes and a reference color chart. But the consequence is that we always need these intermediaries to choose and share colors. Hopefully, in day life someone can say that he loves Yellow without the need of a color code agreement. No matter if it's imprecise !

So, i decided to write a better "humanized" color scheme. I kept the most common colors name and added shades from these colors according to a simple design pattern from Lightness. We can approximately picturing what a Red-Pale, Yellow-Black or Violet-light represents, use this names without confusion and without necessarily using color code.
